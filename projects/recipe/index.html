History

Shortbread was created by accident somewhere in the 12th century when leftover bread dough was left out to dry and become a "biscuit." The butter replaced the yeast and the "biscuit" evolved into Shortbread, the term short came from the texture that butter gave it. This was only for special occasions and for the rich as butter was a luxery. The more refined version of Shortbread is attributed to Scotland as it was popular there.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup caster white sugar
  • 2½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup butter, room temp
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 300 degrees F (150 degrees C).
  2. Mix together flour, butter, and 1/2 cup sugar in a bowl with your hands until well combined.
  3. Press dough into an ungreased 11 ½ x 7 ½-inch sheet tray or jelly roll pan. Sprinkle with 1 tablespoon sugar.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven until edges are light brown, 20 to 30 minutes.
  5. Cut shortbread into squares in the pan while still warm. Cool completely before removing squares from the pan.
